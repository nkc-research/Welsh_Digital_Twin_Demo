Minimum Viable Twin (MVT)

Using Virtual Population Data to Transform Infrastructure Planning

Overview

The Minimum Viable Twin (MVT) combines data from **StatsWales**, the Office for National Statistics (ONS), and NHS Wales to create a synthetic yet realistic representation of regional populations. This digital twin empowers councils, healthcare providers, and planners to simulate scenarios, model future developments, and make data-driven decisions.

How Synthetic Data Represents Real Populations

The MVT uses synthetic data generation techniques to create virtual populations. While the data is not tied to any specific individual, it is derived from aggregated public datasets such as:

  • Population demographics from StatsWales and ONS.
  • Health statistics from NHS Wales.
  • Energy efficiency and housing data for specific regions.

The synthesis process involves aligning real-world statistics, such as age distributions, employment rates, and health conditions, to generate a population that mirrors the real demographics of an area.

Why this is valuable: The synthetic population allows comprehensive analysis without compromising individual privacy while retaining statistical accuracy.

Key Features

  • Data Integration: Combines demographic, health, and geographical data from StatsWales, ONS, and NHS Wales.
  • Virtual Population: Models synthetic individuals with realistic attributes, including age, gender, employment status, and health conditions.
  • Dynamic Updates: Integrates updated datasets for evolving scenarios such as population shifts or new healthcare challenges.

Use Cases

The MVT offers a range of applications for councils and the NHS:

  • Healthcare Planning: Map health conditions and simulate demand for medical services in specific regions.
  • Infrastructure Development: Model housing and energy demand to support sustainable development goals.
  • Transportation Modelling: Analyse commuting trends and improve public transport efficiency.
  • Emergency Response: Identify vulnerable populations and enhance disaster preparedness.

Example: Dynamic Energy Efficiency Insights

Using data from StatsWales, councils can monitor and act on housing energy efficiency trends:

  • Region A: 50% of households with energy ratings below C. Recommendation: Prioritise insulation programmes.
  • Region B: High prevalence of flats with inefficient heating systems. Recommendation: Implement community heating projects.

Dynamic Data Loading

The MVT supports integration of updated statistics and live data feeds, allowing real-time decision-making. For example:

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Track trends in healthcare utilisation or housing demand.
  • Scenario Modelling: Simulate the impact of policy changes, such as increased public transport funding or energy retrofit programmes.

Benefits

  • Data-Driven Insights: Enable councils and healthcare providers to base decisions on evidence, improving outcomes.
  • Privacy Preservation: Synthetic data ensures no individual is identifiable while retaining statistical accuracy.
  • Long-Term Planning: Supports scenario-based analysis for future-proofing infrastructure and healthcare strategies.

How It Works

The MVT uses the following methodology to create and maintain synthetic yet representative populations:

  • Data Collection: Public data sources, such as StatsWales, are used to gather regional statistics.
  • Synthetic Generation: Virtual populations are created using statistical models that align with real-world demographic distributions.
  • Dynamic Updates: The system can integrate new datasets, such as census updates or live NHS data, to maintain relevance.

Conclusion

The Minimum Viable Twin provides a powerful tool for councils and the NHS to engage in both real-time and long-term planning. By using synthetic yet statistically accurate data, decision-makers can improve resource allocation, enhance resilience, and plan for future growth and challenges.
